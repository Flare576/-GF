To accomplish all of your dailies with a net-positive GEC and admiral refresh, do the following:
  • Open Alliance menu, tap donate/Ok 5 times (15 Points)
  • Engage in 15 battles (15 Points)
  • Return your tank to dock each time, transfer ships to dock, and repair from dock (15 Points if Repair Ships available)
  • Loot the debris from each. If you don't reach 120k minerals, mine until complete (15 Points)
  • Complete all 3 of your legion missions (15 Points)
  • Build 10 ships (15 Points)
  • Use Resources
    • 300,000 Minerals (Big ships, Station upgrades, etc.) (15 Points)
    • 30,000 Crystals (Big ships, Research, etc.) (15 Points)
  • Use Items + Use GEC lvl1 (20 Points)
    • Check your daily list for what sort of Resource Item is necessary.
    • Go into Items - Resources and find the 10Gec item.
    • Buy and Use 3 times.
    • If you have items stocked, use 2 of them, or Buy and Use 2 more times.
  • Accept the Free Items 5 times.** (15 Points)
  • Open Navy Acadamy, use a refresh token. (10 Points)
  • *If listed* Probe 3 systems* (15 Points)
  • *If listed* Scan 8 Planets (consider starting a legion) (15 Points)
  • *If listed* Buy 8 items from Alliance Store Note: Ships don't count(15 Points)
  • *If listed* Alliance Help (Just click the help button for 5 members! (15 Points)

* 60 Minute refresh

"Tanking" is a method of fighting in which you direct all damage to a single (Tank) fleet. Generally, you want this fleet made of of Frigates, but as you get stronger you may want to mix in Destroyers.

Ideally, when fighting, you'll have one Tank fleet, one fleet with your strongest ships, and then any other fleets made up of the rest of your strong ships.

Begin by moving all of your combat fleets to VERY close proximity to the target. Once all fleets arrive, initiate combat with ONLY your Tank fleet. Once the battle starts, the other fleets will auto-engage.

After combat concludes you will only have your Tank fleet damaged. If you want to continue fighting, move your non-tank fleets close to the next target, but (if you can) bring your tank back to your station.

When your tank fleet arrives DO NOT REPAIR IT. Tap on each ship type in the fleet and then tap "Send to Dock", then use the slider to re-stock the fleet.

Occasionally, you'll want to then tap on "Dock" on the top and initiate repair from there; you can still only have 1 repair going at a time, but this way you don't ALSO lock down a fleet.

You will then fly your tank fleet out to the target (or tap the target and initiate attack with the tank if the other fleets have already arrived), and repeat the process.

  • Failing a Legion doesn't consume your legion points; Push yourself to aim high!
  • 3-5 player legions yield more Alliance rewards than 2-3 player.
  • The payout of a legion is divided among the participants based on contribution, but it's always better to have the MINIMUM number of participants.
  • You can select a legion enter it, join it, then disband it. Doing so will generate a new, random legion of similar type, letting you get just the right difficulty/size/type/etc.

Legions yield two different types of rewards, which are calculated differently:
  • Alliance Points: take the displayed value on the right and multiply it by the maximum participants. The result is the total points the actual participants will split, based on their grades. Grades are assigned, as far as I can tell, by mystical incantations and blood sacrifices.
  • Alliance Minerals: take the displayed numbers and add them together. The result is the amount of minerals that will be added to the alliance resources
Additionally, since each rank/size legion has the same reward, we can generalize the legions like this, from most rewarding to least (in terms of alliance resources):

* Resources do NOT scale. The "Per person" value is calculated based on the assumption that you always run legions with the MINIMUM participants in order to maximize the number of legions run. Running a legion with more than the minimum will result in the SAME resources (say, 10.63M), but less per- person (10.63M divided among 6 people is less than 10.63M divided among 5)
  • Be aware of fleet speed. Fleets move as fast as their slowest ship type, so if you want all of your fleets to arrive at about the same time, ensure all of them have at least 1 of the same type of ship.
  • When upgrading your station, you'll get an extra build slot.
  • When helping build Alliance structures, you can only send on Workship per building.
  • You can retreat a fleet from a battle by tapping on it from the battle screen, then tapping the "Return" button. Doing so will immediately move the fleet back to your station, but cause 10% damage. This can be a fast way to return from long distances... at a price.
  • Stabilizing fleets cannot move... but they can fight back.
  • NEVER jump into an enemy system while they are guarding the gate. No ammount of synchronization will save you from devastating losses.
  • The 1,000 Station Jump Charge item will add 1/5 of your jump energy back.

The level of your core is based on the levels of the modules installed into it. When on the "Install" tab of the Core interface, pay attention to the symbol in the middle; the order on the Install tab may not match the order on the Core tab.

You probably want to focus your Green/Blue/Purple/Gold modules into the Materials Nexus, which will improve your repair times as it levels up.

Based solely on arm-chair theories, it makes more sense to put time into Admiral Damage under Attack than into individual weapons under Weapons, at least until you start hitting level 20 and get better returns on the faster times.
